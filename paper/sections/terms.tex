\subsection*{Framework terms}

\begin{description}
\item[A=1 (Discrete Causal Lattice)]
   The framework established in Paper~I (\emph{Geometry First}).
   Physics emerges from three axioms applied to a bipartite octahedral
   lattice: \emph{Unity} (amplitude is conserved at every tick, hence
   $\mathcal{A} = 1$), \emph{Locality} (information propagates only
   between adjacent nodes), and \emph{Phase} ($U(1)$ on a per-particle
   internal clock).

\item[Audit table]
   A table mapping the paper's claims to the evidence backing each
   one; each row has a status in
   $\{\texttt{PASS}, \texttt{PART}, \texttt{STUB}, \texttt{FAIL}\}$.
   Canonical: prose elsewhere in the paper does not contradict it.
   See Table~\ref{tab:audit}.

\item[Diamond progression]
   The family of bipartite octahedral lattices $\{\Lambda_d\}_{d \geq 2}$
   generated by the d-of-(d+1) regular simplex vertex construction.
   Custom variant; distinct from chemical diamond.  $\Lambda_3$ is
   Paper~I's substrate lattice.

\item[d-of-(d+1) simplex vertex construction]
   The combinatorial recipe for the diamond progression: take a
   regular $(d+1)$-simplex in $\mathbb{R}^{d+1}$, project to
   $\mathbb{R}^d$, and use $d$ of its $d+1$ vertices as the
   neighbour offsets at each site of $\mathbb{Z}^d$.

\item[\texttt{dcl-delta-p-min}]
   The companion repository housing the empirical investigation of
   $\delta p_\min$.  Section~\ref{sec:dimensional_selection}'s
   corollary $d_\max = 1/\delta p_\min - 1$ is a mathematical
   statement; the question of whether $\delta p_\min$ equals $1/4$
   (or another specific value) is empirical and lives in that repo.

\item[\texttt{dcl-formalism} (planned)]
   Planned sympy-extending package operationalising selected theorems
   of this paper as machine-checked primitives.

\item[\texttt{dcl-zoo}]
   The catalogue repository enumerating the $71$-dimensional per-site
   automorphism algebra at $d = 3$.

\item[Hilbert's Sixth Problem]
   Problem 6 from Hilbert's 1900 list: \emph{``treat in the same
   manner, by means of axioms, those physical sciences in which
   already today mathematics plays an important part.''}  The planned
   capstone paper (\texttt{dcl-paper-XX-hilbert-sixth}) consumes
   this paper's continuum-limit machinery
   (\S\ref{sec:continuum_limits}) and dimensional-selection result
   (\S\ref{sec:dimensional_selection}) for its axiomatisation
   claim.

\item[Hilbert-Sixth on-ramp]
   Informal label for \S\ref{sec:continuum_limits} -- the
   discrete-to-continuum limit machinery the capstone consumes.

\item[Joint-tick rule]
   Paper~I's rule for advancing the two bipartite colour classes of
   $\Lambda_d$ in synchrony at every tick.  Plays into the
   $d_\max$ proof of \S\ref{subsec:dp_min_dmax_corollary} via the
   per-site probability-slot count.

\item[Motivating example]
   In this paper's usage, a section whose content states a claim or
   research direction without proving it.
   Section~\ref{sec:motivating_examples} (Boltzmann + fluid limit)
   consists of motivating examples.  Their audit-table rows are by
   design \texttt{STUB} and stay \texttt{STUB} until subsequent
   papers close them.

\item[Paper~I, Paper~II, Paper~III]
   First three papers of the A=1 series.  Paper~I (\emph{Geometry
   First}, doi:10.5281/zenodo.20078529): substrate priority; lattice
   carries Lorentz structure.  Paper~II (\emph{Geometry Forces
   Physics}): SM gauge group from single conservation axiom.
   Paper~III: tidal ionization and quantum Roche limit.

\item[Paper~IV (working name)]
   The user's working name for this paper in
   \texttt{physics-research/notes/raw\_thoughts.md}.  Title above is
   \emph{Foundations of the A=1 Discrete Causal Lattice: the Diamond
   Progression in Arbitrary Dimension} -- the series-position label
   ``IV'' lives in `raw\_thoughts.md` rather than in the paper title
   itself.
\end{description}

\subsection*{Mathematical terms}

\begin{description}
\item[Automorphism algebra (per-site)]
   The Lie algebra of infinitesimal automorphisms of $\Lambda_d$ that
   fix a chosen site and preserve the bipartite structure and the
   joint-tick rule.  At $d = 3$, $71$-dimensional (catalogued in
   \texttt{dcl-zoo}); generalisation to arbitrary $d$ is the subject
   of \S\ref{sec:automorphism_algebra}.

\item[Counting measure]
   The basic measure $\mu_\#$ on $\Lambda_d$ assigning $|A|$ to a
   finite subset $A$.

\item[Discrete codifferential]
   Formal adjoint $\mathrm{d}^\ast$ of the discrete exterior
   derivative $\mathrm{d}$.

\item[Discrete connection]
   A choice of edge-valued data (a discrete 1-form, abelian case)
   used to define parallel transport along lattice paths.

\item[Discrete curvature]
   The 2-form on plaquettes measuring the failure of plaquette
   holonomy to be the identity.

\item[Discrete exterior calculus]
   The discrete analogue of exterior calculus (forms, $\mathrm{d}$,
   $\mathrm{d}^\ast$, Stokes, Hodge) on the lattice cell complex.

\item[Discrete exterior derivative]
   The operator $\mathrm{d} \colon \Omega^k \to \Omega^{k+1}$
   defined as a signed boundary sum.  Upright $\mathrm{d}$ avoids
   notational collision with the dimension $d$.

\item[Discrete $k$-form]
   A function on oriented $k$-cells of $\Lambda_d$.

\item[Discrete-to-continuum limit]
   The limit $\epsilon \to 0$ of the rescaled lattice
   $\epsilon \Lambda_d$ towards $\mathbb{R}^d$, together with the
   limits of operators / function spaces / measures defined on the
   rescaled lattice.  See \S\ref{sec:continuum_limits}.

\item[Haar-analogue measure]
   On $\Lambda_d$, a measure invariant under the per-site
   automorphism algebra; uniqueness up to scaling.

\item[Holonomy (lattice / plaquette)]
   The product of discrete connection 1-forms along a closed lattice
   path.  Plaquette holonomy is the holonomy around an elementary
   plaquette.

\item[Hyperoctahedral group]
   $B_d$: signed permutations of the $d$ coordinate axes; order
   $2^d \, d!$.

\item[Lattice spacing]
   The parameter $\epsilon > 0$ used to rescale
   $\Lambda_d \subset \mathbb{Z}^d$ to $\epsilon \Lambda_d \subset
   \mathbb{R}^d$.

\item[Sobolev-analogue space]
   $\ell^{k,p}(\Lambda_d)$: discrete functions with discrete
   derivatives (via $\mathrm{d}$) up to order $k$ in $\ell^p$.
\end{description}

\subsection*{Diamond-progression-specific terms}

\begin{description}
\item[Coordination ($\text{coord}(d)$)]
   Number of neighbours of any site in $\Lambda_d$.
   Theorem~\ref{thm:coord_2d}: $\text{coord}(d) = 2d$.

\item[d-of-(d+1) construction]
   See \emph{diamond progression}, \emph{d-of-(d+1) simplex vertex
   construction}.

\item[Even / odd parity]
   The bipartite colour classes of $\Lambda_d$ are
   $V_d^+ = \{x \in \mathbb{Z}^d : \sum_i x_i \equiv 0 \pmod 2\}$
   and $V_d^-$ its complement.

\item[Maximum hop]
   The longest single edge in $\Lambda_d$ (in Euclidean distance
   under the standard simplex projection).
   Theorem~\ref{thm:max_hop}: maximum hop $= \sqrt{d}$.

\item[Unit cell]
   The smallest sub-region of $\Lambda_d$ that tiles the lattice by
   translation.  Volume given by Theorem~\ref{thm:unit_cell_volume}:
   $(d+1)^{(d-1)/2}$.
\end{description}

\subsection*{Dimensional-selection terms}

\begin{description}
\item[$d_\max$]
   Maximum dimension admissible to the A=1 framework under the
   hypothesis $\delta p_\min > 0$.  Theorem~\ref{thm:dp_min_dmax}:
   $d_\max = 1/\delta p_\min - 1$.  Corollary~\ref{cor:dmax_3}: at
   $\delta p_\min = 1/4$, $d_\max = 3$.

\item[Dimensional selection]
   The phenomenon by which a non-zero $\delta p_\min$ forces an upper
   bound on the lattice dimension.  The candidate mechanism that
   selects $d = 3$ rather than permitting it.

\item[$\delta p_\min$ (minimum probability quantum)]
   The hypothesised minimum probability per site in the A=1
   framework.  This paper takes $\delta p_\min > 0$ as a hypothesis
   and derives the $d_\max$ corollary.  The empirical investigation
   of $\delta p_\min$'s existence and value lives in
   \texttt{dcl-delta-p-min}.
\end{description}

\subsection*{Kinetic-theory terms (motivating examples)}

\begin{description}
\item[Chapman-Enskog expansion]
   Asymptotic expansion of the kinetic distribution function $f$ in
   powers of a small parameter, used to derive fluid equations from
   the Boltzmann equation.

\item[Collision operator]
   The operator $Q[f]$ on a kinetic distribution function $f$.

\item[Fluid limit]
   The limit in which a kinetic equation (Boltzmann) gives way to a
   fluid equation (Navier-Stokes or Euler) under an appropriate
   scaling.  Motivating example in
   \S\ref{subsec:fluid_limit}.

\item[$H$-functional]
   The convex functional $H[f] = \sum f \log f$ on kinetic
   distribution functions.

\item[$H$-theorem]
   Boltzmann's theorem (continuum) that $H[f]$ is non-increasing
   under the kinetic dynamics.  Lattice analogue conjectured in
   \S\ref{subsec:boltzmann}; proof deferred.

\item[Navier-Stokes equations]
   The continuum fluid equations; target of the lattice-Boltzmann
   fluid-limit ansatz in \S\ref{subsec:fluid_limit}.
\end{description}
