**Purpose:** Pin the combinatorial definition of the diamond

## Setup

The *diamond progression* is the family
$\{\Lambda_d\}_{d \geq 2}$ of bipartite octahedral lattices
generated by the d-of-(d+1) regular simplex vertex construction.
This note is the working space for the construction's combinatorial
definition.

Paper~I defines the bipartite octahedral lattice in 3-D as the
substrate of A=1 physics.  Verifying that $\Lambda_3$ recovers
Paper~I's lattice is a Phase~2 deliverable
(\texttt{Proposition~\ref{prop:specialisation_paper_i}}).

## Argument

### Provisional construction

Working definition (refine before Section~2 of the paper commits):

$\Lambda_d$ is the graph with:

- **Vertex set:** $V_d = \mathbb{Z}^d$.
- **Bipartition:** $V_d = V_d^+ \sqcup V_d^-$, where
  $V_d^+ = \{x : \sum_i x_i \equiv 0 \pmod 2\}$ and $V_d^- =
  \{x : \sum_i x_i \equiv 1 \pmod 2\}$.  (Strict even / odd parity
  on the coordinate sum.)
- **Edges:** generated by the d-of-(d+1) regular simplex vertex
  offsets.  Take a regular $(d+1)$-simplex in $\mathbb{R}^{d+1}$,
  project to $\mathbb{R}^d$, and use $d$ of its $d+1$ vertices as
  the neighbour offsets at each site.

The specific offset set (which $d$ of the $d+1$ vertices) and the
specific projection (which of two natural choices into
$\mathbb{R}^d$) are open questions; both must be locked before
the three theorems' proofs begin.

### Claims to establish (Phase 2)

1. The bipartition is well-defined for all $d \geq 2$ and every
   edge crosses colour classes.
2. The $d = 3$ instance $\Lambda_3$ is graph-isomorphic to Paper~I's
   bipartite octahedral lattice (cite Paper~I's explicit
   description).
3. $\Lambda_d$ carries a natural action of the hyperoctahedral
   group $B_d$ (signed permutations of coordinate axes), respecting
   the bipartition.

### Three theorems (Section~3 of the paper)

The construction's geometry is characterised by three result-density
theorems (proofs deferred to
\texttt{paper/sections/three\_theorems.tex}):

- $\text{coord}(d) = 2d$
- max Euclidean hop = $\sqrt{d}$
- unit-cell volume = $(d+1)^{(d-1)/2}$

These are the structural spine of Paper~IV.  Each is targeted at
v1.0 \texttt{PASS}; at v0.2 all three are \texttt{STUB} (Phase~2
deliverable).

### Connection to the dimensional-selection result

Combined with a non-zero minimum probability quantum
$\delta p_\min$ (a hypothesis investigated empirically in
`dcl-delta-p-min`), the diamond progression admits the corollary

$$d_\max = \frac{1}{\delta p_\min} - 1.$$

At $\delta p_\min = 1/4$, $d_\max = 3$ -- the lattice cannot
sustain the A=1 unity axiom in $d \geq 4$ because some node
would have to carry less than $\delta p_\min$.  The proof
(Section~9 of the paper) relies on counting the per-site
probability slots; the explicit count ($d + 1$ slots, each
$\geq \delta p_\min$) is the Phase~2 verification target.

## Open questions

1. **Offset set.**  Which $d$ of the $d+1$ simplex vertices?
   Standard convention (e.g.\ omit the apex) vs equivalent
   alternatives.  Pin before Theorem~1 proof.
2. **Projection convention.**  Two natural projections of the
   regular $(d+1)$-simplex into $\mathbb{R}^d$.  Verify they give
   the same max-hop value $\sqrt{d}$ (Theorem~2).
3. **Edge weighting / metric.**  Where does the edge weighting
   enter -- in the combinatorial setup (Section~2) or in
   Section~5 when integration by parts demands a metric?  Decide
   once.
4. **Slot count in the $d_\max$ proof.**  Verify the $d + 1$
   probability slots invoked in
   Theorem~\ref{thm:dp_min_dmax}'s proof sketch is precise.
   Alternative counts (e.g.\ $2d + 1$ via the coordination
   counting) would change the $d_\max$ formula.
5. **Contrast with chemical diamond.**  Spell out the precise
   difference (chemical diamond has coord 4 at all $d$; diamond
   progression has $2d$).  Connection to the "diamond" name --
   shared "tetrahedral / simplex vertices" connotation but distinct
   $d$-dependence.
