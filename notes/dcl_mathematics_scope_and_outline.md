## Setup

`dcl-mathematics` houses Paper~IV of the A=1 Discrete Causal Lattice
(DCL) series.  Paper~I established the bipartite octahedral lattice
at $d = 3$ as the substrate of physics; this paper generalises to
all $d \geq 2$ via a single construction (the *diamond progression*),
proves three result-density theorems characterising the geometry,
and derives a corollary that *selects* $d = 3$ rather than permitting
it.

## Argument

Scope decisions locked at v0.2:

1. **The construction is named.**  *Diamond progression* (custom
   variant, distinct from chemical diamond) -- the family
   $\{\Lambda_d\}_{d \geq 2}$ generated by the d-of-(d+1) regular
   simplex vertex construction.  Strict bipartite via even/odd
   coordinate-sum parity.  Do not silently rename in prose.
2. **Three theorems are the spine.**  $\text{coord}(d) = 2d$;
   max hop = $\sqrt{d}$; unit-cell volume = $(d+1)^{(d-1)/2}$.
   Adding a fourth result-density theorem is fine; removing one is
   a scope decision.
3. **Dimensional selection is the headline corollary.**  Coupling
   the diamond progression to a hypothetical $\delta p_\min > 0$
   gives $d_\max = 1/\delta p_\min - 1$; at $\delta p_\min = 1/4$,
   $d_\max = 3$.  This is the prize.  The mathematical statement
   lives in Section~9 of this paper; the empirical investigation
   of $\delta p_\min$ lives in `dcl-delta-p-min`.
4. **Dimension symbol.**  $d$ (matches the user's working language
   in `raw_thoughts.md` and the d-of-(d+1) construction's natural
   notation).  The discrete exterior derivative uses upright
   $\mathrm{d}$ to avoid notational collision.  *v0.1 used $n$;
   v0.2 corrected.*
5. **Paper-only.**  No code, no experiments.  The sympy
   operationalisation of the three theorems belongs to the planned
   `dcl-formalism` package; the empirical investigation of
   $\delta p_\min$ belongs to `dcl-delta-p-min`.
6. **Motivating-examples policy.**  Boltzmann + NS / Euler appear
   in Section~10 as *framing only*.  Their audit-table rows are
   by design `STUB` and stay `STUB` until subsequent papers close
   them.  Do not promote them.
7. **Deferral boundaries.**  Out of scope:
   - The aspirational F4 sequel ("A=1 forces linear coordination")
     -- holding for after Paper~IV lands.
   - The aspirational prize-of-prizes (generator-fractal
     unification of the diamond progression via IFS / Bratteli /
     operad) -- aspirational; later.
   - The web essay companion ("the progression is fractal") --
     could ship on `dcl-website-geometry-induced-physics-org`
     ahead of Paper~IV; not in scope here.
   - Pinning $\delta p_\min$'s value -- `dcl-delta-p-min` Phase~1
     theoretical pass + the planned discrete-probability paper.
8. **Publication decision.**  Deferred.  Paper format load-bearing
   as series' canonical internal reference regardless of external
   peer review.

## Open questions for Phase~2

1. The explicit offset set used in the d-of-(d+1) simplex vertex
   construction.  The construction is determined up to a choice of
   which $d$ vertices of the $(d+1)$-simplex to use as offsets;
   pick the natural canonical choice and lock it before Theorem~1
   proof.
2. The standard projection of the regular $(d+1)$-simplex into
   $\mathbb{R}^d$ used in Theorem~2 (max hop = $\sqrt{d}$).  Two
   natural projections; verify they give the same max-hop value.
3. The exact slot-count in the dimensional-selection proof of
   Theorem~\ref{thm:dp_min_dmax}.  Currently sketched as ``$d+1$
   slots, each $\geq \delta p_\min$''; Phase~2 work must verify
   this is precise rather than approximate.  Alternative readings
   give different $d_\max$ formulae; the formula
   $d_\max = 1/\delta p_\min - 1$ is the user's stated result from
   `raw_thoughts.md` 2026-05-21 \texttt{[result-2]} and is the
   target.
4. Verify that $d = 3$ recovers the 71-dim per-site algebra
   catalogued in `dcl-zoo` -- specialise the closed-form
   $\dim \mathfrak{a}_d$ to $d = 3$ and check it produces $71$.
