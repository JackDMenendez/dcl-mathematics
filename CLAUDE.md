## What This Project Is

`dcl-mathematics` houses Paper~IV of the A=1 Discrete Causal Lattice
(DCL) series: the foundational mathematics paper proving the
properties of the **diamond progression** -- the family of bipartite
octahedral lattices in dimensions $d \geq 2$ generated by the
d-of-(d+1) regular simplex vertex construction.  Paper~I established
the $d = 3$ case as the substrate of physics; this paper
generalises, proves three result-density theorems characterising
the geometry, and derives the dimensional-selection corollary
$d_\max = 1/\delta p_\min - 1$ (which, with $\delta p_\min$ pinned
by `dcl-delta-p-min`, forces $d = 3$).

No code lives here -- the sympy operationalisation of selected
theorems belongs in the planned `dcl-formalism` package; the
empirical investigation of $\delta p_\min$ lives in
`dcl-delta-p-min`; the physics applications are their own paper
repos.  This repository proves the structural mathematics.

The repository is *paper-only*.  Publication decision deferred;
the paper-shape is load-bearing as the series' canonical internal
mathematical reference regardless of external peer review.

**Title:** *Foundations of the A=1 Discrete Causal Lattice: the
Diamond Progression in Arbitrary Dimension*

**Working alternative (shorter):** *The Diamond Progression:
Bipartite Octahedral Lattices in Arbitrary Dimension from
d-of-(d+1) Simplex Vertices*

load-bearing claim is that **the bipartite-lattice geometry of A=1

diamond progression), is characterised by three clean theorems,
and is *dimensionally selected* by $\delta p_\min$**.  The progression
to $d = 3$ is not by choice -- it is by force of $\delta p_\min = 1/4$.

User's working name in raw_thoughts: "Paper~IV."  The series
numbering (Paper~I, Paper~II, Paper~III) puts this as the fourth
paper; the title above uses *Foundations* rather than "Paper~IV"
because the methodological-capstone naming pattern
(*Geometry First*, *Geometry Forces Physics*, ...) is reserved for
the verb-shaped methodological papers.  Paper~IV is foundations-
shaped.

## Conventions

- **Status legend.** `PASS` / `PART` / `STUB` / `FAIL` (defined in
  the front-matter of `paper/main.tex`).
- **File naming.** Sections: `paper/sections/<topic>.tex`.  Figures:
  `paper/figures/<name>.{tex,pdf,png}` with `.tex` fragment + binary
  pair.  Notes: `notes/<topic>.md`.
- **Cross-references.** Always `\label{}` + `\ref{}` / `\autoref{}`,
  never hard-coded numbers.  Section labels: `sec:<name>`.  Subsection:
  `subsec:<name>`.  Equation: `eq:<name>`.  Figure: `fig:<name>`.  Table:
  `tab:<name>`.  Theorem: `thm:<name>`.  Definition: `def:<name>`.
  Lemma: `lem:<name>`.  Proposition: `prop:<name>`.
- **Bibliography.** All cites flow through `paper/paper-bib/references.bib`.
  Style: `\bibliographystyle{unsrt}` (numeric, in citation order).
- **Dimension symbol.**  Use `$d$` for the lattice dimension throughout
  (matches the user's working language in `raw_thoughts.md` and the
  d-of-(d+1) construction's natural notation).  *Updated 2026-05-21;
  v0.1 used `$n$`.*  The discrete exterior derivative uses upright
  `$\mathrm{d}$` to avoid the notation collision.
- **Diamond progression.** The d-of-(d+1) simplex vertex construction
  is the *diamond progression*.  Distinct from chemical diamond;
  custom variant.  Do not silently rename in prose.
- **Motivating-example tagging.** Boltzmann + NS sections are
  explicitly *motivating examples* in this first paper, not proof
  targets.  Use `\todomark{motivating-example: ...}` to mark prose
  whose load-bearing content is deferred to a subsequent paper.
- **British/American spelling.** The series mixes both; do not
  normalise.  Use British forms (-ise, -ation, neighbour, colour)
  when in doubt -- consistent with issue drafts 001-006 in
  `dcl-project`.
